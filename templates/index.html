About the UPSC Exam

The Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) conducts the Civil Services Examination (CSE) to recruit for top-tier services like IAS, IPS, IFS, IRS, and others. The exam is widely regarded as one of the toughest in India, requiring a deep understanding of diverse subjects, clarity of thought, and excellent problem-solving skills.

Minimum Criteria to Pass the UPSC Exam

  • Age Limit: 21 to 32 years (for general category, relaxable for reserved categories)
  • Education: A bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognized university
  • Physical and Medical Standards (for services like IPS)
  • Clearance of all stages of the exam (Prelims, Mains, and Interview)

UPSC Exam Stages & Details

Prelims

  • Paper 1: General Studies (GS) ‚Äì 200 marks
  • Paper 2: Civil Services Aptitude Test (CSAT) ‚Äì 200 marks (qualifying)

Mains

  • Format: Descriptive (Written Papers)
  • Total Papers: 9 papers (including General Studies, Optional Subject, and Essay)
  • Marks: 1750 marks total for all papers
  • Core Evaluation: Assesses in-depth knowledge across various subjects
  • Language Papers: Includes a paper for Indian language (qualifying)
  • Optional Subject: Candidate‚Äôs choice of subject (decides specialization)

Interview

  • Format: Personality Test (Conducted by UPSC panel)
  • Marks: 275 marks
  • Evaluation: Soft skills, ethics, decision-making, and mental agility
  • Purpose: Tests candidates‚Äô personality and suitability for civil services
  • Criteria: Marks in Mains and Interview combined decide the final ranking
